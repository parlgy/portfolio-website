Project 2

Bizup Application

An online platform where students can buy and sell second hand products. Because the case study was time sensitive, I resolved to conducting Design Sprint as a way to come up with a quick solution creatively while involving everyone who was part of the project.

Duration: 1week

Methods: Design Sprint

Tools: Figma, sticky notes, white board

Role: Sprint Facilitator, UX / UI Designer

Context

The fact that there exists a number of online platforms where people can buy and sell products,the client insisted that We come up with a way to attract people to use this application more often. This was going to be a challenge and therefore I needed to do a lot of initial research including competitive analysis with the existing products. Due to the fixed timeframe, I decided to conduct a design sprint which was to involve everyone in the project.

Design Sprint

Understand/ Map
Ideate/Sketch
Decide
Prototype
Test

Planning The Sprint

Everybody involved in the project was to take part in the sprint. The client provided me with the team and I was the facilitator of the Sprint. I prepared a design sprint Brief which was given to everybody one day before the sprint in order for them to be familiar with the process.

The Sprint Brief

1. Understand

Map

It was important that everyone in the team to have knowledge about the problem that was to be solved. We had light talks concerning the problem and the CEO also emphasized on how solving the problem would bring value to the company. This would help everyone from not diverting from the main objective. The marketing team had already data that they collected from various students during their user research which was an epic thing because it also helped us have insights on our target users.

Key Findings and insights:

  • Students wanted a ready market where they can find many products.
  • They wanted a platform where they can bargain prices of the products.
  • Sellers wanted to have a way to tack the number and type of customers they have.
  • Students also had items that they wanted to sell.
    • 2.Ideate

    Sketch

    In day 2, we used the knowledge of the problem statement we generated previously to come up with with solution. Each individual was to come up with a solution and this was to be done using a pen and paper since we had among us people with no knowledge on design tools. Everyone was to participate because each individuals idea was very important. Everyone was to arrange his/her solutions to form a storyboard which he/she had to explain.

    3. Decide

    This was one of the most important phase as we were to choose a solution that we would prototype to test with users. The decision on what solution to be chosen was to be agreed by everyone and therefore it was important for everybody to participate.We were also to look at how our solutions might conflict our objectives and goals. Each individuals idea was to be reviewed carefully.

    Some of the solutions that we generated included:

  • We decided to include stores because one store could provide many items providing market to multiple users. A store owner was to have a different account and subscription plan than the normal user account.
  • We included a chat section where the buyers would bargain the prices of products with the sellers.
  • We provided the ability of normal users to upload their items too.
    • 4. Prototype

    I as the sole designer took the solutions that we come up with an came up with a high fidelity mockup which was given to the developers who come up with a simple version of the application that we would test with users. The prototype only contained features of the application that we thought were the best for user testing.

    5. Test

    Validate

    This was the last day of the sprint. We gathered some users and one by one had them use the prototype. We were very keen to observe areas that they were interested most with and areas they skipped. This was to enable us to make adjustments to what might suit the users.

    Key findings

  • Most users skipped signup section in order to try out the application first.
  • Most users stopped to read the onboarding pages and therefore they needed to be more informative.
  • Most users rushed to see if they could bargain for prices of items.
    • Iterate

    We observed the reactions of the users and made some adjustments to the applications. These adjustments included adding an informative onboarding pages. We continued with this process as the users tested the application until we were confident with the results.
