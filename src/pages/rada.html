Project 1

Rada Application

RADA is a student developed mobile health application that helps students access information about sexual and reproductive health, professional counseling and peer counseling services and a helpline when in an emergency situation within the University.

Duration: 1month 2weeks

Methods: Interviews, Card sort, Wire-frame and prototyping.

Tools: Adobe XD, Usability hub, InVision, figma

Role: User Research, Visual Design

Context

During the COVID-19 pandemic, people were sent to their homes and therefore it was very difficult for students to physically get counselling services. Therefore, I was tasked with coming up with a mobile application that would allow students to get access to counselling services as well as get easily understandable information on different mental issues and how to overcome them. The students would also link to different counsellors through the application. This would also help students With low self-esteem who are not able to physically approach counsellors for advice.

Design Process

Research

1. Research

Interviews

With the goal of understanding what the students(users) may want to be included in the application, I interviewed 12 students each from different year of study. I wanted to understand how students answers would vary depending on their Year of study, and by so my findings revolved around these main issues i.e. financial issues, relationship issues, Academic Issues and Family issues. Also, with the help of people in the counselling department, I was able to gather insights tha would enable me to come up with an objective that I needed to solve by the end of the case study.

Key findings and Insights

The data I collected help me understand the needs and feelings of the students and basically try to answer te question "how should i build the application".

Some of the key findings included:

  • Keep it Simple.
  • Users should be able to complete tasks quickly.
  • Easy navigation.
  • Users should feel safe while using the application.

    • User Stories

    Finally, I created user stories that I could use to better understand the potential needs of the application to help decide on the functional requirements of the Rada App and that I could refer back to throughout the project to keep focused.

    2. Define - problem statement

    Problem Statement

    Students going through mental challenges need to access almost all counselling services that they would have gotten if it were a physical session with a counsellor. By this, they need to feel safe before they share information to in the app.

    User personas

    Since one of the key findings in my user research was the fact that students of varying year of study have different needs, four personas were created. I revisited these user personas often to remind myself of the needs of my users and maintain a user-centric focus for the duration of the project.

    Mental Model

    Using all the research I collected, I created a mental model for each persona in order to understand how the user might interact with the app, and to create more intuitive user experience.

    User flow and Task analyses

    Finally I created a task analyses and user flows for each persona. This gave me a clear picture of the steps the user would need to take to get to the final goal and how to deliver this goal in the most effective manner as possible.

    Sitemap

    I conducted a card sort to gain insights as to how users might expect rhe content to be organized and displayed, and use the result to create a sitemap.

    3. Ideate

    Low fidelity wire-frames

    Since simplicity and ease of use are one of the biggest aims in Rada application, the number of screens were kept to a minimum and I wanted to focus on highlighting the core features. I started with a pen and paper wireframes and created multiple versions of each screen until I found the combination of features and elements that I thought matched the user needs and that would be as intuitive as much as possible. Next I created a mid-fidelity wireframes in Adobe XD, created a clickable and gave some of my friends to test them out.

    3. Test

    Usability Testing

    This phase was a game-changer - by conducting usability tests, I was able to refine what users were finding useful and completely change up what they did not react well to.

    Key Findings

    I made notes of the positive and negative feedback, so that i knew areas to keep expanding upon and what needed to be reworked.

    Positive

    1. -Users were able to complete task quickly.
    2. -Navigation proved easy to understand

    Negative

    1. -There was too much information that the users find overwhelming.
    2. -Users found icons and texts too small.
    4. Iterate

    Design Changes

    I made changes based on user feedback and then reached out for additional feedback from some fellow designers. I created a newer high-fidelity version of the app with larger icons and clearer texts.
